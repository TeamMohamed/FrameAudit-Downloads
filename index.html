Why this exists

When footage is disputed, visuals alone are not enough.

A recording can look believable while still containing continuity breaks, timing drift, compression inconsistencies, or structural anomalies. FrameAudit examines how a recording behaves across time, structure, motion, and encoding, then packages that assessment into a repeatable review bundle suited to litigation, internal investigations, regulatory scrutiny, and public-interest disputes.

Multi-signal engine

Corroborated signals, not one opaque score.

The product is strongest when it explains how a finding is supported. Each signal contributes to a reviewable consistency profile rather than a black-box verdict.

Continuity and Frame Structure

Examines frame-to-frame behavior for discontinuities, drift, and structural breaks.

Motion and Scene Behaviour

Flags transitions that do not behave like a naturally continuous recording.

Timeline Consistency

Checks whether sequence, timing, and adjacent signals align across the review window.

Compression Irregularities

Looks for encoding changes and compression patterns that deserve closer review.

Audio Continuity

Uses supporting audio behavior to strengthen or weaken a finding window.

Spatial Persistence

Tracks whether anchors and scene structure persist the way a continuous clip should.

Who this is for

Best fit for high-stakes buyers who need a reviewable record.

  • Large companies handling internal investigations, misconduct allegations, disputes, or reputational incidents.
  • Claimant-side civil teams pursuing actions against governments, police forces, or other public bodies.
  • External counsel, expert-support teams, courts, tribunals, and public inquiries dealing with contested recordings.
  • Insurers, risk teams, regulators, and oversight bodies that need a structured evidence package rather than a raw model score.
  • Journalists, NGOs, and verification units working on serious public-interest matters where chain of reasoning matters.

Who it is not for

Not designed for low-context or mass-volume use.

  • Casual consumer disputes where the issue does not justify a structured technical review.
  • Teams seeking instant automated verdicts without analyst review, scope confirmation, or case context.
  • Bulk moderation or high-volume social screening workflows where speed matters more than defensible reporting.
  • Matters where no original recording, timeline, or dispute context can be supplied for review.
  • Buyers looking to replace a full forensic laboratory outright rather than secure a focused disputed-video workflow.
